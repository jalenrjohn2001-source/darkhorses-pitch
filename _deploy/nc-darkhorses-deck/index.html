03The foundation under all of it

Before any of this: become a real business.

A website makes you look like an organization. An LLC (or nonprofit) makes you one — on paper, in the eyes of the law, the bank, and every grant office. It does two big things. Toggle to see what changes.

1. Protection

Your house is on the line

2. Credibility & money

Capped at bake sales

Plain version: No entity = you're personally responsible if something goes wrong, and you're locked out of grants, foundations, and a real business bank account. With an entity (and a tax ID), you can open a program bank account, accept tax-deductible donations, and apply for the funding that's reserved for actual organizations. The big national leagues bundle nonprofit eligibility in for exactly this reasonS.
